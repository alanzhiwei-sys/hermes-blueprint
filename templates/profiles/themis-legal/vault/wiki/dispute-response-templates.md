## 1. Client Dispute Response Letter Template

Use this when a client sends a demand, complaint, or threat of legal action. **Goal:** acknowledge professionally, preserve your legal position, avoid admissions, and buy time.

---

**[Your Letterhead]**

**Date:** [DD/MM/YYYY]

**To:** [Client Name]
[Client Address]

**Re: Response to Your Letter Dated [Date] — Project: [Address/Ref]**

Dear [Client Name],

We refer to your letter dated [date] regarding [briefly describe the issue — e.g., "alleged defects in the smart home installation at your residence"].

We take all client concerns seriously. We are currently reviewing our project records, including the signed Contract dated [date], Variation Orders, site inspection reports, and all correspondence between our team and yourself.

We will provide you with our substantive response within **[14] calendar days**.

In the interim, we note the following without prejudice to our full rights and remedies:

1. **Scope of Works:** Our obligations are defined in the Contract and any agreed Variation Orders. We respectfully note that [brief factual observation without admitting fault — e.g., "the items referenced in your letter were not part of the original signed Scope of Works" OR "we have attended to this issue on [date] as reflected in our service report attached"].

2. **Defect Liability Period:** The Contract provides a [12]-month Defect Liability Period commencing [DD/MM/YYYY]. Any valid defect claim within this period will be addressed in accordance with Clause [X] of the Contract.

3. **Dispute Resolution:** The Contract provides for [describe — e.g., "mediation before litigation"]. We remain willing to discuss the matter directly and, if necessary, to mediate through the Singapore Mediation Centre.

This letter is written without prejudice to our rights, which we expressly reserve. Nothing in this letter constitutes an admission of liability.

Pending our full response, we remain committed to resolving this matter fairly and in accordance with our contractual obligations.

Yours sincerely,

**[Your Name / Company Name]**

---

### Usage Notes — Risk Flags

| Element | Severity | Note |
|---------|----------|------|
| "Without prejudice" | ⚠️ HIGH | **Must include** — protects the letter from being used against you in court as an admission. |
| No admission of fault | ⚠️ HIGH | Never say "we apologise for the error" or "we accept responsibility" in this letter. |
| 14-day response window | ⚡ MEDIUM | Reasonable. Shorter may be impractical; longer may escalate. |
| Reference to DLP | ℹ️ LOW | Shows you know your contract and are operating within it. |

## 2. Supplier / Sub-Contractor Dispute Escalation Template

Use when a subcon delivers defective work, abandons the job, or disputes payment.

---

**[Your Letterhead]**

**Date:** [DD/MM/YYYY]

**To:** [Sub-Contractor Name]
[UEN/Address]

**Re: Notice of Breach — Sub-Contract Agreement Dated [Date] — Project: [Address/Ref]**

Dear [Sub-Contractor Name],

**Step 1: State the Breach**

Pursuant to the Sub-Contract Agreement between us dated [date] (the "Agreement"), you are in breach of your obligations:

- **Clause [X] — Scope of Works:** [describe — e.g., "The carpentry installation at the above premises does not conform to the specifications in Appendix A. Specifically: (list defects with photos/evidence)."]
- **Clause [Y] — Timeline:** [describe — e.g., "Works were to be completed by [date]. As of today, [describe status]."]

**Step 2: Demand Cure**

Pursuant to Clause [Z], you are hereby given **[7] calendar days** from the date of this letter to remedy the above breach. Our specific requirements are:

1. [Specific action — e.g., "Remove and re-install the defective cabinets in accordance with specifications by [date]."]
2. [Specific action — e.g., "Provide a revised works schedule with daily milestones."]

**Step 3: State Consequences**

If the breach is not remedied within the cure period:

- We will engage a replacement contractor to complete/rectify the works.
- All costs incurred will be deducted from amounts otherwise payable to you.
- We reserve the right to terminate the Agreement and claim damages for any shortfall.

This notice is issued without prejudice to our rights under the Agreement and at law.

Yours sincerely,

**[Your Name / Company Name]**

---

### Escalation Ladder

| Stage | Action | When |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | Verbal discussion / WhatsApp | First sign of issue |
| 2 | Formal letter (above) | Verbal resolution fails |
| 3 | Mediation (SMC) | Letter response unsatisfactory |
| 4 | Small Claims Tribunal (≤$20K) or litigation | Mediation fails or refused |

## 3. Regulatory Notice Response Template

Use if you receive a notice from PDPC, CASE (Consumers Association of Singapore), BCA, HDB, or any Singapore regulatory body.

---

**[Your Letterhead]**

**Date:** [DD/MM/YYYY]

**To:** [Regulatory Body]
[Address]

**Re: Response to Your Notice Dated [Date] — Reference: [Notice Ref No.]**

Dear Sir/Madam,

We acknowledge receipt of your notice dated [date] (Ref: [number]) regarding [subject].

We are taking this matter seriously. We have commenced an internal review and are gathering all relevant records, including:

- [List relevant records — e.g., contract documents, correspondence, site reports, data protection policies]

We will provide our full response within **[timeframe stated in the notice, or 14 calendar days if not specified]** .

If you require any interim information, please contact the undersigned directly at [phone/email].

Yours faithfully,

**[Your Name / Designation]**

---

### Critical Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Acknowledge within 24 hours | Ignore or delay beyond deadline |
| Seek legal advice for PDPC/BCA notices (⚠️ HIGH) | Respond with unverified statements |
| Preserve ALL records — do not delete anything | Destroy or alter documents after receiving notice |
| Cooperate professionally | Be adversarial — regulators have broad powers |

## 4. Small Claims Tribunal (SCT) Preparation Checklist

The Singapore Small Claims Tribunal hears claims up to **SGD 20,000** (or up to SGD 30,000 if both parties consent). It is designed to be lawyer-free, fast, and inexpensive.

### Pre-Filing Checklist

- [ ] **Contract is in writing?** SCT requires a written contract or clear documentary evidence. Oral agreements are harder to prove.
- [ ] **Claim ≤ SGD 20,000?** If higher, consider waiving the excess or proceeding in the Magistrate's Court.
- [ ] **Within limitation period?** 6 years from the date the cause of action accrued (Limitation Act, Cap. 163).
- [ ] **Attempted mediation?** SCT may ask whether you have tried to resolve the dispute directly.

### Evidence Gathering Checklist

| Evidence Type | What to Collect | Notes |
|---------------|----------------|------|
| **Contract & VOs** | Signed contract, all Variation Orders, acceptance emails | ⚠️ HIGH — foundation of any claim |
| **Payment Records** | Bank transfers, receipts, invoices, payment schedule | Shows who paid what and when |
| **Correspondence** | WhatsApp logs, emails, SMS | Export complete threads; include dates. Screenshot timestamps. |
| **Site Evidence** | Dated photographs, videos, site inspection reports | Time-stamped. Before/after comparisons ideal. |
| **Third-Party Reports** | Independent inspection reports, expert opinions | Adds credibility. May require expert fees. |
| **Defect Log** | Chronological list of defects reported + responses | Shows you followed the DLP process |
| **Witness Statements** | Written accounts from anyone present at key events | Signed and dated |
| **Timeline** | Chronology of events in table format | Makes the narrative easy for the Registrar to follow |

### Hearing Day Checklist

- [ ] Bring 3 copies of all documents (Tribunal, other party, yourself)
- [ ] Arrive 30 minutes early (State Courts, 1 Havelock Square)
- [ ] Dress professionally (no legal gowns — SCT is informal but respectful)
- [ ] Prepare a 2-minute opening summary: the contract, the breach, the loss, the remedy
- [ ] Be ready to answer: "What have you done to try to settle this?"
- [ ] Bring your witness(es) if any

### SCT Flow

1. **File online** via the Community Justice and Tribunals System (CJTS)
2. **Consultation** before a Registrar (informal, settlement-focused)
3. **Hearing** before a Tribunal Referee (if consultation fails)
4. **Order** — enforceable as a District Court order

### ⚠️ HIGH Risk: Filing Without Evidence

The SCT is document-driven. If you cannot produce a signed contract and clear evidence of breach/damages, your claim is unlikely to succeed regardless of how "unfair" the outcome feels.

**Revision:** Before filing, complete the Evidence Gathering Checklist above. If the checklist is incomplete, do not file — negotiate, mediate, or gather more evidence first.

## 5. Quick-Reference: Severity & Action Matrix

| Scenario | Severity | First Action | Fallback |
|----------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Client complaint (contractual) | ℹ️ LOW | Client Dispute Response Letter | Mediation |
| Subcon abandoned works | ⚠️ HIGH | Supplier Dispute Escalation + engage replacement | SCT / litigation |
| PDPC notice received | ⚠️ HIGH | Regulatory Notice Response + engage PDPA lawyer | — |
| CASE complaint | ⚡ MEDIUM | Regulatory Notice Response + prepare evidence | CASE mediation |
| Client files SCT claim | ⚠️ HIGH | Evidence gathering + file response within timeline | Hearing |
| Demand letter from lawyer | ⚠️ HIGH | Do not ignore; seek legal advice; respond via lawyer | — |
