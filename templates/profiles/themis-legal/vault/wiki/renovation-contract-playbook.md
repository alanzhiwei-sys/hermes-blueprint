# Renovation Contract Playbook

**Jurisdiction:** Singapore | **Profile:** Themis-Legal | **Last Updated:** 2026-06-10

---

## 1. Standard Renovation Contract Structure

A Singapore renovation contract should include these **minimum required sections**:

| Section | What It Covers |
|---------|---------------|
| Parties & Premises | Full legal names, NRIC/UEN, property address, project duration (start & end) |
| Scope of Works (SoW) | Itemised schedule with quantities, materials, brand/model specs. Vague SoWs are ⚠️ HIGH risk |
| Price & Payment Schedule | Total contract sum, milestone-linked instalments (see §3) |
| Variations (VO) | Trigger conditions, pricing method, approval chain (see §2) |
| Defect Liability Period (DLP) | Minimum 12 months; scope and exclusions (see §4) |
| Delays & Liquidated Damages | Per-day LD rate, grace period, force majeure carve-outs |
| Termination | Grounds, notice period, cure window, consequences (see §5) |
| Dispute Resolution | Mediation → SCT (≤$20K) → arbitration/litigation |
| Governing Law | **Republic of Singapore** — must be explicit |

### Clause → Risk → Revision: Missing SoW Particulars

- **Clause:** _"Contractor shall supply and install kitchen cabinets as per discussion."_
- ⚠️ **HIGH** — Unenforceable. No material specs, dimensions, or finish identified. Any dispute becomes he-said-she-said.
- **Revision:** Attach appendix with cabinet dimensions (L×W×H in mm), plywood grade (e.g., "18mm Birch Ply, Grade MR"), laminate brand/colour code, and hardware brand/model for hinges and runners.

## 2. VO (Variation of Price) Clause Audit Checklist

Variation orders are the #1 source of renovation disputes in Singapore. This checklist catches gaps before sign-off.

### Audit Checklist

- [ ] **VO trigger defined?** Clause must state: "No variation shall be effective unless signed in writing by both parties."
- [ ] **Pricing method stated?** Unit rate, lump sum, or cost-plus with a cap. ⚠️ HIGH if open-ended cost-plus.
- [ ] **Approval chain documented?** Who from the client side approves (owner only, ID, or either)?
- [ ] **VO cap?** Consider a ceiling — e.g., "Aggregate variations shall not exceed 10% of the original contract sum without a new agreement."
- [ ] **Timeline impact addressed?** Each VO must state the additional days it adds to project completion.
- [ ] **Payment terms for VO?** Is the VO amount due immediately or folded into the next milestone? (Recommendation: fold in.)

### Clause → Risk → Revision: Open-Ended VO

- **Clause:** _"Contractor may carry out additional works as instructed and charge accordingly."_
- ⚠️ **HIGH** — Blank cheque. No client approval gate; no price cap; no timeline disclosure.
- **Revision:** _"No variation order (VO) shall be binding unless (a) a VO form stating scope, cost, and additional calendar days is signed by both parties; and (b) the aggregate of all VOs does not exceed 10% of the original contract sum without a separate supplemental agreement."_

## 3. Payment Milestone Best Practices

### Recommended Milestone Structure (Interior Renovation)

| Milestone | % of Contract Sum | Trigger |
|-----------|------------------|---------|
| 1. Deposit | 10% | Upon signing |
| 2. Commencement | 20% | First day of hacking/demolition |
| 3. Carpentry / Wet Works | 30% | Completion of carpentry & tiling |
| 4. Handover | 30% | Practical completion; keys returned |
| 5. DLP Expiry | 10% | 12 months after handover, defect-free |

### Key Principles

- **Hold 10% until end of DLP.** ⚠️ HIGH if the contract pays 100% before DLP ends — you lose all leverage on defects.
- **Milestone triggers must be objective.** "Upon completion" is vague. Specify: "Upon written confirmation of completion, accompanied by dated photographs."
- **No milestone should exceed 30% of total.** Concentrates too much risk.

### Clause → Risk → Revision: Full Payment at Handover

- **Clause:** _"Balance 40% payable upon handover of keys."_
- ⚡ **MEDIUM** — No retention for DLP. If defects emerge in month 3, the client has no payment leverage.
- **Revision:** _"5.1  10% of the contract sum (Defect Retention Sum) shall be withheld from final payment and released only upon expiry of the 12-month Defect Liability Period, provided all notified defects have been rectified."_

## 4. Defect Liability Period (DLP) Standards

### Minimum: 12 Months

Singapore industry norm is **12 months** from the date of practical completion (handover). Many HDB-licensed contractors default to this. Opt for **24 months** where possible.

### What the DLP Must Cover

- [ ] Workmanship defects (tile popping, paint peeling, water seepage)
- [ ] Material defects (laminate delamination, hinge failure)
- [ ] Installation defects (misaligned doors, leaking plumbing joints)
- [ ] Rectification timeline (e.g., within 14 days of written notice)
- [ ] Consequence of non-rectification (client may engage third party; cost deducted from retention sum)

### Clause → Risk → Revision: No DLP Clause

- **Clause:** (no DLP mentioned)
- ⚠️ **HIGH** — Common law warranties still apply, but enforcement requires litigation. No contractual DLP = no expedited rectification right.
- **Revision:** _"8.1  The Contractor warrants all works for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of practical completion (the Defect Liability Period). 8.2  Defects notified in writing during the DLP shall be rectified within fourteen (14) calendar days at the Contractor's sole cost. Failure to rectify entitles the Owner to engage a third-party contractor and deduct the cost from the Defect Retention Sum."_

## 5. Termination Triggers

### Grounds for Termination (Owner-Initiated)

- [ ] Contractor insolvency, winding-up, or judicial management
- [ ] Abandonment of works (> 14 consecutive days without progress)
- [ ] Material breach uncured after 7 days' written notice
- [ ] Works not in accordance with regulatory requirements (BCA, HDB, SCDF)
- [ ] Assignment or sub-contracting without consent

### Clause → Risk → Revision: No Cure Period

- **Clause:** _"Owner may terminate immediately for any breach."_
- ⚡ **MEDIUM** — Too harsh; may be voided under the Unfair Contract Terms Act (UCTA). A short cure period strengthens enforceability.
- **Revision:** _"The Owner may terminate this Agreement by seven (7) days' written notice if the Contractor fails to remedy a material breach within seven (7) days of receiving written notice of such breach."_

## 6. Sub-Contractor Agreement Template (Summary)

Every sub-contractor (subcon) should sign a short-form agreement covering:

1. **Scope & Specifications** — reference the main contract SoW
2. **Price & Payment** — tied to main contract milestones
3. **Insurance** — WICA (Work Injury Compensation Act) compliance, public liability ≥ SGD 1M
4. **Indemnity** — subcon indemnifies main con against all claims arising from subcon's works
5. **Defect Liability** — back-to-back with main contract DLP
6. **Non-solicitation** — subcon shall not deal directly with the client for 12 months
7. **Governing Law** — Singapore

### Clause → Risk → Revision: No Insurance Requirement

- **Clause:** (no insurance clause in subcon agreement)
- ⚠️ **HIGH** — If subcon worker is injured on site, main contractor bears liability under WICA if subcon is uninsured.
- **Revision:** _"The Sub-Contractor shall maintain at all times: (a) Work Injury Compensation insurance as required by the Work Injury Compensation Act (Cap. 354); and (b) Public Liability insurance of not less than SGD 1,000,000 per occurrence. Certificates of insurance shall be provided to the Main Contractor before commencement of works."_
