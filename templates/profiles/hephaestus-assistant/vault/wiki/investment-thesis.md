# Investment Thesis

A personal investment thesis is your North Star — a written statement that anchors every allocation decision, prevents emotional trading, and keeps you accountable. This document provides the framework for building and maintaining yours.

## Decision Framework

Before committing capital, run every opportunity through this four-question filter:

1. **Conviction check** — Can you explain the thesis to a 12-year-old in two sentences? If not, don't invest.
2. **Horizon match** — Does the holding period this investment demands align with your actual liquidity needs? Short-term money has no business in long-duration assets.
3. **Downside-first** — What is the maximum permanent loss scenario? Frame it in absolute SGD terms, not percentages.
4. **Exit criteria** — Under what conditions will you sell? Write this *before* buying. "It went down 20%" is not a thesis — it's panic.

## Risk Tolerance Self-Assessment

| Factor | Conservative | Moderate | Aggressive |
|--------|-------------|----------|------------|
| Age to retirement | < 5 years | 5-15 years | > 15 years |
| Emergency fund | 3 months | 6 months | 12+ months |
| Max acceptable drawdown | 10% | 25% | 40%+ |
| Reaction to -20% month | Sells everything | Reviews calmly | Buys more |

Score yourself honestly. Your portfolio should let you sleep at night. Risk tolerance isn't aspirational — it's behavioral.

## Asset Allocation (Singapore Context)

**Core Portfolio Skeleton:**

- **Tier 1 — Capital Preservation (30-50%)**: CPF SA/OA top-ups, Singapore Savings Bonds (SSB), T-bills. These are your "never lose" allocations. SSBs offer step-up interest with government backing and instant redemption at par.
- **Tier 2 — Income Generation (20-35%)**: S-REITs (Mapletree, Capitaland, Frasers), high-quality dividend equities (DBS, OCBC, Singtel), and CPFIS-approved bond funds. Focus on distribution consistency over yield-chasing.
- **Tier 3 — Growth (15-30%)**: Global equity ETFs (VWRA, CSPX), thematic baskets, and — cautiously — individual equities where your circle of competence is genuine.

**Rebalancing Rule**: Review allocations quarterly. Trigger a rebalance when any tier drifts more than ±5 percentage points from target.

## Singapore-Specific Vehicles

### Singapore Savings Bonds (SSB)
Government-guaranteed, no capital loss, redeemable any month at par + accrued interest. Ideal for emergency fund tiers 2-4 (months 4-12) and near-term sinking funds. Current applications via DBS/POSB, OCBC, or UOB ATMs and internet banking. The $200k individual cap makes this a meaningful portfolio building block.

### CPF Investment Scheme (CPFIS)
Allows deploying OA and SA funds beyond the default 2.5%/4.0% floors. *Only* use this if you have conviction you'll beat CPF's risk-free returns after fees. The CPFIS gate (OA: first $20k cannot be invested; SA: first $40k) is a feature, not a bug — it protects you from yourself. Approved products include unit trusts, ETFs, and selected equities listed on SGX.

### S-REITs (Singapore Real Estate Investment Trusts)
Tax-transparent vehicles distributing 90%+ of taxable income. Key metrics beyond yield: gearing ratio (regulatory cap 45%, most quality REITs run 30-38%), interest coverage ratio (>4x is comfortable), and sponsor quality. Favourable tax treatment — individual investors pay 0% on distributions.

## Action Steps

1. Write your personal investment thesis in one paragraph. Revisit quarterly.
2. Map current holdings to the three-tier framework and identify drift.
3. Set up a recurring SSB ladder (apply every 1-2 months for dollar-cost averaging across rate cycles).
4. If using CPFIS, calculate the breakeven return needed over CPF guaranteed rates. If unsure, don't invest.
