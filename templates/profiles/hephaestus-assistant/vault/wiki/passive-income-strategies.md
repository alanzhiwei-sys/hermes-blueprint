# Passive Income Strategies

Passive income isn't truly passive — every stream demands upfront effort, ongoing maintenance, or both. This guide catalogues viable strategies for a Singapore-based professional, ranked by the effort/reward calculus that matters.

## Strategy Deep Dives

### 1. SSB/CD Ladder (Capital Preservation Tier)
Build a 12-month ladder of Singapore Savings Bonds and fixed deposits. Each month, one tranche matures — roll it forward into the best available rate. This isn't wealth-building; it's sleep insurance. At current SSB rates, a $100k ladder generates ~$250/month. The real yield is psychological: knowing 12 months of expenses are liquid and government-backed.

**Action**: Open a CDP account if you haven't. Apply for SSB through DBS/POSB, OCBC, or UOB internet banking during the monthly window (typically first week of each month).

### 2. S-REIT Dividend Portfolio (Income Generation Tier)
S-REITs are Singapore's most accessible income engine. A diversified basket of 6-8 REITs across retail, industrial, office, and hospitality sub-sectors can yield 4-7% annually. Reinvest dividends during accumulation, shift to cash distributions in drawdown.

**Watchlist**: Mapletree Logistics Trust, CapitaLand Integrated Commercial Trust, Frasers Centrepoint Trust, Keppel DC REIT (data centre exposure). Monitor gearing (stay below 38%) and interest coverage (above 3.5x).

**Tax advantage**: Individual investors pay 0% tax on S-REIT distributions regardless of income bracket. This is a structural edge.

### 3. Digital Products (Scalable Tier)
Create once, sell indefinitely. Templates (Notion, Excel, Canva), online courses (Udemy, Teachable, Gumroad), and niche tools command margins north of 80%. The Singapore angle: produce in English for a global audience, but consider bilingual (EN/Chinese) products to capture the regional premium.

**Minimum viable product**: One 30-minute course on a narrow, high-demand skill → publish on Gumroad → promote via a single LinkedIn post. Measure: 100 views × 2% conversion × $19.90 = $39.80 in the first week. Iterate from there.

## Singapore Tax Considerations

Passive income is generally **not taxable** for individuals in Singapore unless it constitutes a trade or business:

- **Dividends**: Exempt under the one-tier corporate tax system (companies pay tax, shareholders receive dividends tax-free).
- **Interest (SSB, bank deposits)**: Not taxable for individuals.
- **Rental income**: Taxable at your marginal rate, but deductible expenses (mortgage interest, property tax, maintenance, agent fees) meaningfully reduce the net taxable amount.
- **Digital product sales / consulting**: If recurring and systematic, IRAS may consider this a trade. Track revenue separately and consult a tax professional once monthly income exceeds ~$1,000.

**CPF angle**: Passive income doesn't attract CPF contributions unless it's employment income. This is a structural advantage — every dollar of investment income is take-home, not split between OA/SA/MA.

## Scalability Ladder — Where to Focus

1. **Phase 1 (Month 1-3)**: Build the SSB/CD ladder. This is your foundation. No yield optimisation matters if you can't survive a job loss.
2. **Phase 2 (Month 4-12)**: Deploy capital into a dividend portfolio. Start with $5-10k, add monthly. Use a DBS Vickers or FSMOne account for low brokerage.
3. **Phase 3 (Month 12+)**: Launch one digital product. Ship fast, learn faster. The first product teaches you distribution; the third one makes money.

**Kill criterion**: If any strategy hasn't generated positive cash flow within 12 months of launch (digital products) or deployment (investments), re-evaluate. Don't confuse patience with stubbornness.
