# Wellness Habits

Wellness isn't a luxury you earn after productivity — it's the foundation productivity rests on. Neglect it long enough, and everything else collapses. This guide covers the five pillars that keep your body and mind operational.

## Sleep Hygiene

Sleep is the single highest-leverage health intervention available. Poor sleep impairs decision-making, emotional regulation, and immune function within 48 hours.

**The Sleep Protocol**:

- **Fixed wake time, every day.** Weekends included. Your circadian rhythm doesn't know it's Saturday. Wake at the same time and your body will naturally calibrate bedtime.
- **Wind-down window (60 minutes before bed)** : No screens. Blue light suppresses melatonin by up to 50%. Replace phone scrolling with reading (physical book or e-ink), gentle stretching, or conversation.
- **Temperature**: 18-20°C (65-68°F) is optimal for sleep. In Singapore, aircon at 24-25°C with a fan is the practical equivalent.
- **Caffeine curfew**: No caffeine after 2pm. Caffeine has a half-life of 5-6 hours — that 4pm coffee still has 50% potency at 10pm.
- **Alcohol reality check**: Alcohol might help you fall asleep, but it destroys sleep quality. It suppresses REM sleep and fragments the second half of the night. If you drink, stop at least 3 hours before bed.
- **The 20-minute rule**: If you're awake in bed for 20+ minutes, get up. Go to another room, read something boring (no screens), return when sleepy. Lying awake trains your brain that bed = worry zone.

**Non-negotiable minimum**: 7 hours. Below this, cognitive performance degrades measurably. Below 6 hours, you're functionally impaired — comparable to a 0.05% blood alcohol level.

## Stress Recovery

Stress isn't the enemy — chronic stress without recovery is. The goal isn't zero stress; it's oscillation between stress and recovery.

**Daily recovery micro-habits**:
- **Morning light exposure** (10-15 min within 30 min of waking): Sets your cortisol rhythm. In Singapore, step onto the balcony or take a short walk before checking your phone.
- **Box breathing** (4-4-4-4): Inhale 4 seconds, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4. Three cycles resets your autonomic nervous system. Use it between meetings, before difficult conversations, or when you feel your jaw clench.
- **Transition ritual**: Between work and home, create a deliberate boundary. A 5-minute walk, changing clothes, washing your face — any sensory cue that signals "work mode off, family mode on."

**Weekly recovery**:
- One full day without work. No email, no Slack, no "just checking one thing."
- One activity that produces flow — a state where you lose track of time. This could be exercise, cooking, music, or a hobby. Flow is recovery.

**Burnout pre-cursors** (intervene when 2+ appear):
- Dreading mornings that you used to handle fine
- Cynicism about work/clients/people you normally respect
- Reduced efficiency — same tasks take longer
- Emotional numbness or irritability with loved ones
- Physical symptoms: headaches, GI issues, frequent illness

If you hit 3+ of these for more than two weeks, treat it as a medical issue — not a motivation problem. Reduce obligations. Sleep more. Talk to someone.

## Exercise

Movement is non-negotiable. The goal isn't aesthetics — it's functional capacity and mental health.

**Minimum effective dose**:
- **150 minutes moderate aerobic activity per week** (brisk walk, cycling, swimming) OR **75 minutes vigorous** (running, HIIT, sports).
- **2 strength sessions per week** — bodyweight minimum (push-ups, squats, lunges, planks). Add resistance as you progress.
- **Daily mobility** — 5-10 minutes of stretching or yoga. Your future spine will thank you.

**Singapore-specific options**:
- Park Connector Network (PCN) for running/cycling — free, accessible, scenic
- ActiveSG gyms and pools — $1.50-$2.50 per entry for citizens/PRs
- HDB fitness corners — outdoor bodyweight stations in most estates
- ClassPass for variety without gym lock-in

**The accountability hack**: Schedule exercise like a meeting. It goes in the calendar. It's non-optional. If you wait until you "feel like it," you'll never go.

## Screen Time Management

Screens are tools that have become traps. The average adult spends 3-5 hours daily on their phone — time that could be sleep, exercise, or connection.

**Interventions that work**:
- **Phone outside the bedroom**: Buy an alarm clock. The phone charges in another room overnight. This single change improves sleep, reduces late-night doom-scrolling, and breaks the "check phone immediately upon waking" reflex.
- **Grayscale mode**: Settings → Accessibility → Color Filters → Grayscale. A colorless screen is less addictive — the app designers know this; that's why they use bright colors.
- **App time limits**: iOS Screen Time / Android Digital Wellbeing. Set 30-minute daily caps for social media. When the limit hits, the app locks. No "15 more minutes."
- **Notification purge**: Turn off all notifications except calls, messages from specific people, and calendar alerts. Every buzz is someone else's priority hijacking your attention.
- **One screen at a time**: No phone while watching TV. No laptop while on a call. Single-tasking is a superpower.

## Burnout Signs — The Complete Checklist

Burnout is an occupational phenomenon (WHO ICD-11), not a personal failure. It has three dimensions:

1. **Exhaustion** — Physical and emotional depletion that sleep doesn't fix.
2. **Cynicism/depersonalization** — Feeling detached from your work, clients, or the people you serve. "What's the point?" becomes a recurring thought.
3. **Reduced professional efficacy** — You're working harder but accomplishing less. The quality gap is visible to you, even if others haven't noticed.

**The mirror test**: If a friend described your current state to you, would you be concerned? Answer honestly. Then act on that concern — you'd tell a friend to rest, so rest.

**Recovery protocol**: Reduce work hours immediately. Prioritize sleep. Reconnect with activities that brought joy before burnout. Consider professional support (counsellor, therapist, coach). Recovery takes weeks to months — there is no weekend fix for burnout.

## Action Steps

1. Implement the fixed wake time for one week. Track how you feel.
2. Move your phone charger out of the bedroom tonight.
3. Schedule exercise for the next 7 days as non-negotiable calendar events.
4. Run the burnout checklist. If 2+ signs are present, take one recovery action today — not tomorrow.
