# Singapore Mid-Range HDB Renovation Competitive Analysis
# Living Document — Updated June 10, 2026
# Next Review: September 2026

## Target Segment
- Mid-range HDB (BTO & Resale)
- Budget: SGD $30,000 - $70,000
- 3-4 room to 5-room flats
- Primary battleground: clients who have received 2-4 competitor quotes

---

## COMPETITOR 1: Weiken Home

### Positioning
Mid-range ID firm with established showroom, strong listing platform presence (Qanvast, Renopedia, HomeRenoGuru). Known for reliable carpentry output and structured quoting process.

### Pricing (HDB 4-room resale, full renovation)
- Total: $45,000 - $70,000
- Per sqft: ~$48-$74
- Carpentry: $180-$250/lf (depending on finish grade)
- Tiling: $8-$14 psf (marble-look, mid-range)
- Painting: $2.50-$3.50 psf
- Est. margin: 20-25%

### Strengths
- Brand recognition on listing platforms
- Physical showroom for material selection
- Structured quoting process looks polished

### Weaknesses
- VO reputation: initial quote often needs add-ons for rewiring upgrades, bathroom waterproofing, hardware
- Designer-dependent quality variance (senior vs. junior)
- Transactional feel; multiple sales touchpoints create pressure

### Counter-Pitch (Quick)
"Weiken is solid, but check their past clients for variation orders. My quote covers everything from day one — VOs only if you change your mind."

---

## COMPETITOR 2: UrbanMood

### Positioning
Trend-driven, younger demographic, social-media-first (Instagram/TikTok). Strong on aesthetic styling (fluted panels, ambient lighting, Japandi).

### Pricing (HDB 4-room)
- Total: $40,000 - $60,000
- Per sqft: ~$42-$63
- Carpentry: $160-$220/lf (melamine/veneer)
- Tiling: $7-$11 psf
- Ambient lighting sold as separate add-on ($1,500-$3,000)
- Est. margin: 18-22%

### Strengths
- Aspirational social media content
- Appeals strongly to first-time homeowners and young couples
- Design vocabulary is current and on-trend

### Weaknesses
- Junior designers handle most actual builds; creative director rarely manages sites
- Project management handoff is inconsistent after deal signing
- Under-quotes MEP rough-ins and waterproofing; VOs follow
- Trendy material choices can date quickly

### Counter-Pitch (Quick)
"Great style. But ask who actually manages your build. I personally oversee every project at [YOUR_COMPANY] — no handoff to a junior coordinator."

---

## COMPETITOR 3: Fineline Design Interior

### Positioning
Established mid-range firm, high volume, aggressive marketing, broad service portfolio (reno, furnishing, smart home).

### Pricing (HDB 4-room)
- Total: $39,900 - $65,000 (headline)
- Realistic delivered price: $45,900 - $78,000 (after add-ons)
- Per sqft: ~$42-$68 (headline); ~$48-$82 (delivered)
- "Starting from" promotions are loss leaders; final bill 15-20% higher
- Est. margin: 15-20% on promo pricing; reverts to 20-25% on delivered

### Strengths
- Recognised brand name, especially with older clients
- One-stop convenience (reno + furnishing + smart home)
- Fast turnaround promise for flexible clients

### Weaknesses
- Corporate, impersonal post-signing experience
- Headline "package" pricing excludes flooring, hardware, bathroom accessories, lighting
- Tight timelines sometimes mean rushed finishing details
- High designer turnover; mid-project reassignments reported

### Counter-Pitch (Quick)
"They'll quote $39,900 upfront, then add-ons stack up. My number on page one is your real total. If they're genuinely cheaper after everything, go with them. But that gap is real."

## DIFFERENTIATION MAP FOR HOO NG FATT HENG

### 1. One-Person Accountability
They sign you up, hand you off to a team. You never lose contact.
This is your biggest edge. Mid-range clients don't hire for the logo; they hire to avoid getting burned.

### 2. Quote Comprehensiveness
They leave gaps to close as VOs. You name everything upfront.
Lead with: "Let me show you what usually gets added on later, so you can budget for it properly."

### 3. Personal Site Management
You use ClickUp, iPad/Procreate, actual site walks. Most PMs in this tier don't.
Show them site photos, timelines, trade coordination. Competence persuades.

### 4. No-Pressure Consultation
All three competitors push to close. Speed-close tactics erode trust.
"I'd rather you wait and think than sign something you're uncomfortable with."

### 5. Material Education
Not just selling — teaching.
Explain sintered stone vs. granite. Show tile samples with actual durability specs, not just appearance. Builds trust capital they cannot copy.

## MAPPING STRATEGY

Every time a client asks "How does this compare to [Competitor]?", use this structure:

1. Acknowledge fairly (never trash a competitor)
2. Name their actual strength ("They're good at X")
3. Reveal the gap a client wouldn't know ("But here's what happens after you sign —")
4. Position yourself as the solution ("In my quote, that's already covered")

---

## PRICE OBJECTION HANDLER

When client says "Can you match [Competitor]'s price of $XX,XXX?":

- First: ask for their actual written quote from the competitor
- Second: compare scope line-by-line, not headline numbers
- Third: show what's excluded from their competitor's quote
- Fourth: "I can adjust material grades if budget is the constraint. But let me show you exactly what changes first, so the choice is informed."
