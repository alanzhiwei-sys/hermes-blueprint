# HDB & Condo Project Templates

## Overview
Standardised project templates and compliance guidelines for HDB flats and condominium units. Use these as the foundation for every new project — customise scope, not structure.

---

## HDB Renovation Guidelines

### Flat Types & Common Scopes

#### BTO (Build-To-Order) — New Flat
- **Key Context**: Bare unit; optional HDB flooring package already installed in some units; no existing built-ins to hack
- **Typical Scope**:
  - **Optional**: Overlay vinyl/tiles over HDB-provided flooring (check warranty implications)
  - Electrical: Full rewiring not typically needed; additional points from DB
  - Carpentry: Kitchen cabinets, bedroom wardrobes, TV feature wall, shoe cabinet, study desk
  - Bathroom: HDB-provided sanitary ware; overlay tiles possible (check weight loading)
  - Painting: Full unit, typically 2–3 colours
- **Timeline**: 4–6 weeks
- **Budget Range**: $15K–$35K
- **Key Regulations**:
  - No hacking of structural walls/columns
  - Air-con ledge: compressor only; no enclosures or storage
  - Window grilles: Must use HDB-approved window contractor
  - Renovation must complete within HDB's stipulated period (typically 3 months from key collection)

#### Resale Flat — Full Renovation
- **Key Context**: Existing finishes to hack; potential plumbing/electrical issues from age; possible asbestos in pre-1990 flats
- **Typical Scope**:
  - Demolition: Full hacking of floor/wall tiles, existing carpentry, false ceilings
  - MEP: Full rewiring, plumbing re-piping (common for 20+ year old flats), air-con trunking
  - Wet Works: Full bathroom waterproofing + tiling (both bathrooms), kitchen tiling
  - Carpentry: Full suite — kitchen (top + bottom), 2–3 bedroom wardrobes, TV console, shoe cabinet, feature wall
  - Flooring: Vinyl or tiles throughout (check HDB weight limits for tiles if overlaying existing screed)
  - Painting: Full unit
- **Timeline**: 8–10 weeks
- **Budget Range**: $40K–$80K
- **Key Regulations**:
  - HDB permit required before any hacking works commence
  - Waterproofing must use HDB-approved system with 72-hour ponding test
  - Bathroom floor: allowable hacking within stipulated thickness; reinforce waterproofing membrane
  - All debris disposal via HDB-approved channels
  - No works on Sundays and Public Holidays

### HDB Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Renovation permit approved (via HDB e-Services)
- [ ] RRP (Registered Renovation Contractor) assigned
- [ ] Scope within HDB guidelines (no structural hacking, no alteration to common services)
- [ ] Waterproofing test scheduled with HDB-appointed QP if needed
- [ ] Air-con installation by BCA-trained installer
- [ ] Window works by HDB-approved window contractor
- [ ] Working hours respected (Mon–Sat 8am–6pm; no noisy works before 9am)
- [ ] Debris disposal via designated area; no corridor obstruction

## Condominium Renovation Guidelines

### Key Differences from HDB
| Factor | HDB | Condo |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Governing Body | HDB | MCST |
| Permit Authority | HDB Branch Office | MCST Management Office |
| Structural Works | Generally not allowed | Allowed with PE + BCA submission |
| Working Hours | Mon–Sat 8am–6pm | Varies by MCST by-law (typically Mon–Fri 9am–5pm, Sat 9am–1pm) |
| Renovation Deposit | Not required | Required ($1K–$5K typically) |
| Common Area Protection | Corridor hoarding if needed | Mandatory: lift padding, floor protection, hoarding |
| Noise Restrictions | No noisy works on Sun/PH | Typically no noisy works on Sun/PH + restricted Sat hours |
| Lift Booking | Not required | Required; must book service lift slot for material delivery and debris removal |

### MCST Compliance Checklist
- [ ] MCST renovation application submitted and approved
- [ ] Renovation deposit paid
- [ ] Insurance certificates submitted (public liability minimum $1M)
- [ ] PE endorsement obtained (if structural works)
- [ ] BCA submission via CORENET (if structural)
- [ ] Common area protection: lift padding, corridor floor protection, hoarding at entrance
- [ ] Service lift booking confirmed for each phase (delivery, debris removal)
- [ ] Working hours confirmed with management office
- [ ] Pre-works photos of all common areas (for deposit protection)
- [ ] MCST final inspection scheduled (for deposit refund)

### Condo Renovation Deposit Recovery Checklist
- [ ] All common area protection removed
- [ ] No damage to lifts, corridors, lobby
- [ ] Debris cleared from designated area
- [ ] Management office sign-off obtained
- [ ] Deposit refund applied within 30 days of completion
