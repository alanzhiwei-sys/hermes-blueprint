# Subcontractor Management

## Overview
Systematic framework for vetting, engaging, and managing subcontractors across all renovation trades. Effective subcon management directly impacts project margins, quality, and timeline adherence.

---

## Subcontractor Vetting Process

### Stage 1: Initial Screening
- **Source Channels**: Trade referrals, supplier recommendations, industry WhatsApp groups, site visits to observe active teams
- **Minimum Criteria**:
  - Registered business (ACRA) — verify at `www.acra.gov.sg`
  - Valid insurance (public liability, WICA)
  - Minimum 3 years in trade (exceptions for specialists with strong portfolio)
  - 3 recent project references (within last 12 months)

### Stage 2: Portfolio & Reference Check
- **Portfolio Review**:
  - Photos of 5+ completed projects (not just showroom/pinterest)
  - Ideally site visit to a project in-progress — observe workmanship, cleanliness, pace
- **Reference Calls** — Ask:
  - "Did they complete on schedule?"
  - "How did they handle defects or callbacks?"
  - "Would you use them again?"
  - "Any unexpected costs or scope creep?"

### Stage 3: Trial Project
- Assign a small scope (e.g., one bathroom tiling, one room carpentry)
- Evaluate on: **Quality** (40%), **Speed** (25%), **Communication** (20%), **Cleanliness** (15%)
- Pass threshold: ≥ 7/10 across all four criteria
- Only then graduate to Approved Subcon List

### Approved Subcon Database Fields
```
Name / Company / UEN / Contact / Trade(s) / Rate Card / Quality Score / Reliability Score / Last Project / Notes
```

## Rate Negotiation Framework

### Pre-Negotiation Preparation
- **Know the market rate**: Benchmark against 3 other subcontractors for same trade and scope
- **Volume leverage**: Aggregate projects — "I have 3 bathrooms coming up next month"
- **Payment speed**: Offer faster payment terms (e.g., 7-day nett) in exchange for 5–8% rate reduction
- **Package bundling**: "If you take all the carpentry for this project + the [LOCATION_REDACTED] one, I'll give you both"

### Rate Card Template
```
Trade: [Carpentry / Tiling / Plumbing / Electrical / Painting / Glass]
Subcon: [Name]
Date Agreed: [DD/MM/YYYY] | Valid Until: [DD/MM/YYYY]

| Item | Unit | Rate | Notes |
|------|------|------|-------|
| Kitchen cabinet (standard) | per ft | $XXX | Plywood carcass, laminate finish |
| Kitchen cabinet (premium) | per ft | $XXX | Solid ply, textured laminate |
| Wardrobe (standard) | per ft | $XXX | Sliding/swing door included |
| Feature wall | per sqft | $XX | Fluted panel, backing |

**Terms**: Payment 14 days after invoice | Defect rectification within 5 working days | Rate review every 6 months
```

### Negotiation Guardrails
- **Floor**: Never go below their last agreed rate without additional volume commitment
- **Ceiling**: Max 25% above market median for premium/urgent jobs
- **Red flag**: Subcon who accepts first offer without counter — either too expensive (room to drop) or desperate

## Quality Control System

### On-Site Inspection Checklist
- [ ] **Carpentry**: Check joints (tight, no gaps), laminate edges (no chipping), soft-close mechanisms (all functional), alignment (use spirit level), interior finishing
- [ ] **Tiling**: Tap test for hollowness (<5% of tiles allowed), lippage (<1mm for rectified tiles, <2mm standard), grout consistency, cuts around corners/edges, fall gradient to floor trap
- [ ] **Plumbing**: Pressure test all pipes before concealment, check all joints for leaks (24-hr observation), water flow rate at all outlets
- [ ] **Painting**: 3-coat minimum (1 sealer + 2 finish), check under natural + artificial light, no roller marks/drips, edges cut clean
- [ ] **Electrical**: All points live (test with tester), correct amperage at DB, earth leakage test, RCCB trip test

### Defect Management
- **Snag List**: Documented during final walkthrough; photos + room location + trade responsible
- **Rectification Window**: 5 working days from notification
- **Escalation**: If not resolved within 10 working days, engage backup subcon and deduct from original subcon's retention
- **Quality Score Update**: Adjust subcon's quality rating after each project; below 6/10 triggers review

## Payment Terms

### Standard Terms
- **Milestone-based**: Payment upon completion and inspection of each phase
- **Retention**: 10% of each subcon invoice held for 30 days post-handover (defect period)
- **Invoice requirements**: Must include project reference, trade scope, date completed, itemised breakdown

### Payment Timing
| Priority | Payment Window | Condition |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| Standard | 30 days from invoice | Default for established subcons |
| Preferred | 14 days from invoice | Consistently high quality, >5 projects together |
| Express | 7 days from invoice | For critical-path urgent works, premium rates |

### Red Flag Payment Behaviours
- Subcon requesting full upfront payment → Maximum 20% mobilisation advance
- Subcon demanding cash-only → Require proper invoicing with UEN
- Subcon holding site access hostage for payment → Immediate termination consideration; escalate to [NAME_REDACTED]

## Relationship Management

### Subcon Tier System
| Tier | Criteria | Benefits |
|------|----------|----------|
| Tier 1 (Strategic Partner) | 10+ projects, quality ≥ 9/10, never missed deadline | First offer on all new projects, 14-day payment, annual rate review with 3–5% increment |
| Tier 2 (Preferred) | 5+ projects, quality ≥ 7/10, reliable | Regular work, 30-day payment, semi-annual rate review |
| Tier 3 (Approved) | Passed trial, quality ≥ 7/10 | Project-by-project engagement |
| Tier 4 (Probation) | New or under review | Trial scope only, weekly inspections |

### Seasonal Planning
- **Peak Periods**: CNY (Jan–Feb), post-BTO key collection (varies by estate)
- **Strategy**: Book Tier 1 subcons 4–6 weeks ahead during peak; maintain 20% capacity buffer with Tier 2
- **Lull Periods**: Hungry Ghost Festival (Aug–Sep) — negotiate better rates, schedule training/inspection-intensive projects
