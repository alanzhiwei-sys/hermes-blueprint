# Competitor Intelligence

## Purpose
Ongoing tracking of renovation competitor pricing, positioning, and performance. Use to calibrate Zeus's own quotes, identify market gaps, and sharpen the "Trusted Advisor" sales pitch against competitor weaknesses.

---

## Competitor Landscape Map

### Tier 1: Premium Design Firms
- **Examples**: The Interior Lab, Space Factor, Third Avenue
- **Positioning**: Design-led, high-end finishes, $80K+ projects
- **Strengths**: Brand recognition, Instagram presence, design awards
- **Weaknesses**: Long lead times, less flexible on budget, designer turnover
- **Threat Level**: Low (different market segment); watch for mid-range encroachment

### Tier 2: Mid-Range ID Firms (Direct Competitors)
- **Examples**: Weiken, UrbanMood, Fineline Design
- **Positioning**: Quality renovation with design, $35K–$80K
- **Strengths**: Established pipelines, showrooms, digital marketing
- **Weaknesses**: Can feel transactional, designer-dependent quality variance, scope creep on variation orders
- **Threat Level**: **HIGH** — this is Zeus's primary battleground

### Tier 3: Budget Contractors
- **Examples**: Renopedia-listed contractors, Carousell contractors, JB-based teams
- **Positioning**: Low price, minimal design, $15K–$35K
- **Strengths**: Price, speed
- **Weaknesses**: Quality inconsistency, limited warranty, poor communication, potential for abandonment
- **Threat Level**: Medium (price-sensitive clients); differentiate on trust and quality

### Tier 4: Direct Subcontractors
- **Examples**: Individual carpenters, tilers, electricians
- **Positioning**: Trade-by-trade, lowest cost, client-as-PM
- **Strengths**: Lowest absolute cost
- **Weaknesses**: Zero project management, no warranty coordination, client bears all coordination risk
- **Threat Level**: Low (different value proposition); convert by highlighting PM burden

## Competitor Pricing Tracking

### Data Collection Methods
- **Client Intel**: During consultations, ask clients what other quotes they've received. Note competitor name, scope, and price.
- **Industry Network**: Trade suppliers often share ballpark figures across firms. Build relationships.
- **Public Sources**: Qanvast, Renopedia, HomeRenoGuru — many firms publish indicative pricing
- **Mystery Shopping**: Every quarter, submit a dummy brief to 2–3 competitors and obtain a quote for benchmarking

## Win/Loss Analysis

### Win Factors (Why Clients Choose [YOUR_COMPANY])
Track every won project with primary reason:
- **Trust/Relationship (40%)**: "[NAME_REDACTED] was the only one who explained everything clearly"
- **Price Competitiveness (30%)**: Quote was within budget with better scope than competitor
- **Design Vision (15%)**: Mood boards and Procreate sketches impressed client
- **Referral (10%)**: Recommended by previous client, friend, or family
- **Speed/Responsiveness (5%)**: Faster response than competitors

### Loss Factors (Why Clients Chose Elsewhere)
Track every lost project with primary reason:
- **Price Too High (35%)**: Competitor undercut significantly
- **Designer Rapport (25%)**: Client preferred competitor's designer personality/chemistry
- **Competitor Reputation (20%)**: Client chose a more well-known brand
- **Timeline Mismatch (10%)**: Zeus couldn't meet client's start date
- **Scope Limitations (5%)**: Competitor offered in-house services Zeus outsources
- **Other (5%)**: Location, language, bundled deals (e.g., furniture packages)

### Win/Loss Template (Log After Every Client Decision)
```
Date | Client | Project Type | Decision: WON / LOST | Competitor (if lost) | Primary Reason | Quote Amount | Takeaways
```

### Quarterly Win/Loss Review
- **Target Win Rate**: ≥ 60% for qualified leads
- **If win rate drops below 50%**: Trigger pricing review + competitor re-benchmarking
- **If "Price Too High" > 40% of losses**: Consider re-scoping to offer good-better-best options; adjust margin targets on entry-level package
- **If "Brand" is dominant loss factor**: Invest in social proof (testimonials, before/after content, Qanvast listings)

## Competitive Battle Cards

### Quick Reference: Weiken
- **Known For**: Mid-range, strong carpentry, decent showroom
- **Typical Price**: $45K–$70K for 4RM resale
- **Weakness to Exploit**: Variation order (VO) reputation — clients report scope creep; Zeus's USP: "My quote is comprehensive — what you see is what you pay; VOs only if YOU change your mind"
- **Counter-Pitch**: "Weiken is a solid choice — but check how many VOs their past clients received. I quote the full scope upfront."

### Quick Reference: UrbanMood
- **Known For**: Younger clientele, social media savvy, trendy designs
- **Typical Price**: $40K–$60K for 4RM resale
- **Weakness to Exploit**: Designer assigned may be junior; quality depends heavily on which designer you get
- **Counter-Pitch**: "Great style — but ask who your actual project manager will be. At [YOUR_COMPANY], I personally oversee every project."

### Quick Reference: Fineline Design
- **Known For**: Established firm, large team, aggressive marketing
- **Typical Price**: $45K–$65K for 4RM resale
- **Weakness to Exploit**: Can feel corporate/impersonal; tight project timelines may compromise quality
- **Counter-Pitch**: "They do volume. I do care. You won't be passed to a junior coordinator after signing."

## Market Intelligence Cadence
- **Weekly**: Log quotes received from all client consultations
- **Monthly**: Spot-check 1–2 competitor quotes via mystery shopping or network
- **Quarterly**: Full competitor pricing review — update benchmark ranges
- **Annual**: Competitor landscape reassessment — any new entrants? Any exits?
