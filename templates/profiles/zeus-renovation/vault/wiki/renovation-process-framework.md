# Renovation Process Framework

## Overview
End-to-end process framework for residential renovation projects in Singapore. Covers both HDB flats and private condominiums with distinct compliance paths, permit requirements, and inspection gates.

---

## HDB Renovation Process

### Key Governing Body
- **Authority**: Housing & Development Board (HDB)
- **Portal**: HDB Renovation e-Services via My HDBPage
- **Licensed Contractor Requirement**: All works must be performed by an HDB Registered Renovation Contractor (RRP)

### Permit & Approval Sequence
1. **Pre-Work Assessment** — Client submits renovation intent; contractor verifies flat eligibility (MOP clearance for resale, BSC status for new flats)
2. **Renovation Permit Application** — Filed by RRP via e-Services; includes scope of work, floor plan with markings, estimated timeline
3. **HDB Approval** — Typical turnaround: 3–5 working days for standard works; 7–14 days for demolition of structural walls (requires PE endorsement)
4. **Key Restrictions (HDB)**:
   - **No hacking** of structural walls/columns/beams
   - Wet works (tiling/waterproofing) in bathrooms/kitchen must use HDB-approved waterproofing system
   - No alteration to refuse chute, gas pipes, electrical riser
   - Air-con ledge: compressor only; no enclosures
   - Toilet floor: minimum 72-hour water ponding test post-waterproofing

### Timeline Phases (HDB – Typical 4-Room Resale: 8–10 weeks)
| Phase | Duration | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| Phase 1: Demolition | Week 1 | Hacking of non-structural walls, floor/wall tiles, built-in carpentry removal |
| Phase 2: MEP Rough-In | Weeks 1–2 | Electrical rewiring, plumbing pipe runs, air-con trunking |
| Phase 3: Wet Works | Weeks 2–4 | Waterproofing, tiling, screeding. **Water ponding test at end** |
| Phase 4: Carpentry & Ceiling | Weeks 4–6 | False ceiling, built-in carpentry fabrication and installation |
| Phase 5: Finishing | Weeks 6–8 | Painting, glass/mirror installation, electrical faceplates, plumbing fixtures |
| Phase 6: Cleaning & Handover | Week 8–9 | Chemical wash, defect rectification, final walkthrough |

### Inspection Gates (HDB)
- **Gate 1**: Post-waterproofing — water ponding test witnessed by HDB or appointed QP
- **Gate 2**: Post-tiling — check for hollow tiles (tap test), lippage, grouting
- **Gate 3**: Final — all M&E functional, carpentry defects, paint touch-ups

## Condominium Renovation Process

### Key Governing Body
- **Authority**: Building and Construction Authority (BCA) for structural works; MCST (Management Corporation Strata Title) for by-law compliance
- **Pre-requisite**: MCST renovation deposit (typically $1,000–$5,000 depending on development)

### Permit & Approval Sequence
1. **MCST Submission** — Submit renovation plans, PE endorsement for structural works, proof of contractor insurance (public liability minimum $1M)
2. **MCST Approval** — Typical 5–10 working days; some MCSTs hold monthly council meetings causing longer delays
3. **BCA Submission** (if structural) — Structural plan submission with Qualified Person (PE) endorsement via CORENET
4. **Key Restrictions (Condo)**:
   - MCST by-laws govern working hours (typically Mon–Fri 9am–5pm, Sat 9am–1pm; no noisy works on Sunday/PH)
   - No hacking of structural elements without BCA permit + PE
   - Floor hacking: check slab thickness; condos often have thinner slabs with embedded services
   - Lobby/common area protection: mandatory hoarding/protective sheeting in lifts and corridors

### Timeline Phases (Condo – Typical 3-Bedroom: 10–14 weeks)
| Phase | Duration | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| Phase 1: Protection & Setup | Week 1 | Common area protection, delivery scheduling with management office |
| Phase 2: Demolition | Weeks 1–2 | Hacking (restricted hours), debris removal via designated lift with protection |
| Phase 3: MEP Rough-In | Weeks 2–3 | Electrical, plumbing, air-con. Note: condo DB box capacity may need upgrading |
| Phase 4: Wet Works | Weeks 3–5 | Waterproofing, tiling, screeding. Longer curing due to condo HVAC considerations |
| Phase 5: Carpentry & Ceiling | Weeks 5–8 | False ceiling with cove lighting, built-in wardrobes, kitchen cabinets |
| Phase 6: Finishing | Weeks 8–10 | Painting, fixtures, glass works |
| Phase 7: Cleaning & Handover | Weeks 10–12 | Handover inspection with MCST rep sign-off on common area restoration |

### Inspection Gates (Condo)
- **Gate 1**: MCST pre-works inspection — photograph all common areas for deposit protection
- **Gate 2**: Post-waterproofing — water ponding test
- **Gate 3**: MCST final inspection — verify common area restoration, refund of renovation deposit
- **Gate 4**: Client handover with defect list & 12-month defect liability period

## General Compliance

### BCA Requirements
- **Buildability Score**: Not applicable to residential renovations unless adding GFA
- **Safety**: Workplace Safety and Health (WSH) compliance; risk assessment (RA) for high-risk works
- **Asbestos**: Pre-1990 buildings may require asbestos survey before demolition

### Insurance Requirements
- **Public Liability**: Minimum $1M coverage (mandatory for condo MCST)
- **Work Injury Compensation**: WICA insurance for all workers on site
- **All Risks**: Cover for client's existing property during works

### Dispute Resolution
- HDB: Mediation via HDB Branch Office
- Condo: MCST by-law tribunal via Strata Titles Boards
- Contractual: Singapore Mediation Centre or SCT (Small Claims Tribunal for claims <$20K)

## Checklist: Project Kickoff
- [ ] Flat/unit eligibility confirmed (HDB MOP / MCST approval)
- [ ] Renovation permit applied and approved
- [ ] Insurance certificates obtained and submitted
- [ ] Floor plan with scope of work filed
- [ ] Timeline agreed with milestone dates
- [ ] Payment schedule aligned to phase completion gates
- [ ] Protection works completed (condo common areas)
- [ ] Pre-renovation defects documented with photos
