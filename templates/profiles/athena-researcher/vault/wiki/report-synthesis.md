# Report Synthesis

## Overview

Transforming raw research findings into structured, decision-ready reports. The goal is clarity, actionability, and honest communication of uncertainty — not volume.

## Executive Summary Structure

The executive summary is the most-read section of any research report. It must stand alone and be understandable in under 3 minutes. Follow this rigid structure:

### Five-Paragraph Executive Summary

1. **Context & Objective:** One sentence each — what prompted this research, what question it answers.
2. **Key Findings:** Three to five bullet-point findings, each with an evidence grade (A-D). Bold the most actionable one.
3. **Market / Competitive Snapshot:** One to two sentences summarizing the landscape at a glance.
4. **Recommendations:** Prioritized list (max 3) with estimated impact, effort, and risk.
5. **Critical Uncertainties:** What we don't know and why it matters. If the answer depends on an unverified assumption, state it.

**Length target:** Under 300 words total for the executive summary.

## Finding-to-Recommendation Mapping

Every significant finding should trace to at least one recommendation. Use this mapping table to ensure alignment and prevent orphaned findings:

| Finding | Evidence Grade | Implication | Recommendation | Owner | Timeline |
|---------|---------------|-------------|---------------|-------|----------|
| Finding summary | A/B/C/D | What it means for the business | Actionable next step | Who should act | When |

**Antipatterns to avoid:**
- Findings with no recommendation attached (why did we research this?)
- Recommendations with no finding backing them (are we guessing?)
- All Grade-A findings pointing to trivial actions, Grade-C findings driving major bets

## Evidence Citation

Cite every claim that a reader might question. Use inline citation format:

**Format:** `(Source: [Source Name], [Date], [Tier], [URL if public])`

**Example:** "The market grew 23% YoY (Source: Gartner Market Share Report, Q3 2025, Tier 2, gartner.com/report-id)."

### Citation Rules
- Tier-1 sources (SEC filings, government data): Full citation mandatory
- Tier-2 sources (analyst reports, journals): Author/organization + date + report name
- Tier-3 sources (trade press): Publication name + date
- Tier-4 sources (blogs, opinions): Use sparingly. Flag explicitly: "According to [Author]'s analysis..."

**Citation Checklist**
- [ ] Every quantitative claim has a dated source
- [ ] No "industry experts say" without naming the expert
- [ ] Proprietary research methodology documented and reproducible
- [ ] Source URLs functional (test before finalizing)

## Uncertainty Communication

How to communicate what you don't know without undermining credibility:

### Uncertainty Language Ladder

| Level | Language | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **High Confidence** | "The data shows...", "We can confirm..." | Grade A findings |
| **Moderate Confidence** | "Evidence suggests...", "The pattern indicates..." | Grade B findings |
| **Low Confidence** | "Early signals point to...", "Our working hypothesis is..." | Grade C findings |
| **Speculative** | "One possible scenario is...", "If current trends continue..." | Grade D findings |

### Explicit Uncertainty Statements

At the end of every report, include a brief **Confidence & Caveats** section:

```
**Confidence & Caveats**
- Highest-confidence finding: [X] (Grade A, 3+ independent Tier-1 sources)
- Lowest-confidence finding: [Y] (Grade C, single source, contradictory signals)
- Key assumption: [Z] — if this is wrong, recommendations 2 and 3 change significantly
- Data freshness: Most recent data point is from [DATE]. Fast-moving market may have shifted.
- Known gaps: We could not verify [X] due to [reason]. Recommend follow-up research.
```

## Report Quality Checklist

Before delivering any research report, verify:

- [ ] Executive summary passes the "3-minute standalone" test
- [ ] All findings mapped to at least one recommendation
- [ ] Every quantitative claim has a dated, accessible source
- [ ] Uncertainty language calibrated to evidence grade
- [ ] Confidence & Caveats section present and honest
- [ ] Methodology section documents how research was conducted
- [ ] No marketing language, hyperbole, or unsupported superlatives
