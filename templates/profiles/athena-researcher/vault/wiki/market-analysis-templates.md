# Market Analysis Templates

## Overview

Standardized frameworks and templates for sizing markets, analyzing competitive dynamics, and evaluating market entry opportunities. Use these as starting points — adapt dimensions to your specific industry and research question.

## TAM / SAM / SOM Sizing

The foundational market-sizing hierarchy. Always calculate top-down AND bottom-up, then reconcile.

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
The total global revenue opportunity if you captured 100% of the market.

**Top-Down Approach:**
- Start with industry reports (Gartner, IDC, Statista, IBISWorld)
- Apply segment filters (geography, vertical, company size)
- Document source, year, and growth rate assumption

**Bottom-Up Approach:**
- Estimate total potential customers × average annual contract value (ACV)
- Validate customer count via government business registries, industry association data

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
The portion of TAM reachable with your current business model, geography, and channel strategy.

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)
Realistic short-term (1-3 year) capture target based on competitive position, sales capacity, and growth rate.

**Checklist: Market Sizing Quality**
- [ ] Both top-down and bottom-up calculated — flag discrepancies >30%
- [ ] Source year and methodology documented for each input
- [ ] Growth rate assumptions explicitly stated (CAGR, base year)
- [ ] Geographic and segment boundaries clearly defined
- [ ] SOM benchmarked against known competitor revenues for sanity check

## Porter's Five Forces

Systematic competitive analysis framework. Rate each force as **High / Medium / Low** with evidence.

### 1. Threat of New Entrants
- Capital requirements, economies of scale, switching costs, regulatory barriers, brand loyalty, access to distribution
- **Key metric:** Number of new entrants in past 3 years

### 2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers
- Supplier concentration, availability of substitutes, switching costs, threat of forward integration
- **Key metric:** Number of viable alternative suppliers

### 3. Bargaining Power of Buyers
- Buyer concentration, price sensitivity, switching costs, availability of substitutes
- **Key metric:** Average customer churn rate in the industry

### 4. Threat of Substitute Products
- Relative price-performance of substitutes, buyer propensity to switch, switching costs
- **Key metric:** Cross-price elasticity where available

### 5. Industry Rivalry
- Number of competitors, industry growth rate, exit barriers, differentiation, fixed costs
- **Key metric:** Industry concentration (HHI or CR4)

## Trend Analysis

Categorize trends by impact horizon and evidence strength:

| Horizon | Timeframe | Example Signals |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Emerging** | 3-5+ years | Academic research, patent filings, startup activity |
| **Developing** | 1-3 years | VC funding increasing, early commercial adoption, trade press coverage |
| **Current** | Now | Mainstream adoption, established vendor category, analyst coverage |
| **Declining** | Now | Shrinking revenue, negative sentiment, talent exodus |

For each trend, document: **signal strength** (weak/moderate/strong), **evidence sources**, **estimated CAGR**, and **relevance to the specific research question**.

## Market Entry Assessment

Score each potential market on four dimensions (1-5 scale):

1. **Market Attractiveness:** Size, growth rate, margins, cyclicality
2. **Competitive Intensity:** Concentration, differentiation, incumbent strength
3. **Entry Feasibility:** Regulatory hurdles, capital requirements, talent availability, partner ecosystem
4. **Strategic Fit:** Alignment with existing capabilities, brand, and long-term strategy

**Decision Matrix:** Weight each dimension by strategic priority, multiply scores, rank markets by weighted total.

## Segment Sizing

For multi-segment markets, break down by:
- **Vertical/Industry:** Finance, healthcare, retail, etc.
- **Company Size:** SMB, mid-market, enterprise (define employee/revenue bands)
- **Geography:** Country or region with local GDP/IT-spend benchmarks
- **Use Case:** Different application areas within the market

**Template Output:** Segment name | Est. size ($M) | CAGR | Competitor density | Entry difficulty | Strategic priority
