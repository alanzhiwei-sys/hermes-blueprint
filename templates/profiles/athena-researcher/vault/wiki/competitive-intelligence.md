# Competitive Intelligence

## Overview

Competitive intelligence (CI) is the systematic collection and analysis of information about competitors to inform strategic decision-making. This guide covers frameworks, data-gathering techniques, and actionable outputs.

## Competitor Profiling

Build a structured profile for each key competitor. Capture the following dimensions:

### Baseline Profile Template

```
**Competitor Name:**
- Founded / HQ / Size (employees, revenue band)
- Funding: Total raised, last round, investors, valuation
- Leadership: CEO, key hires, board composition
- Product Lines: Core offerings, pricing model, target segment
- Go-to-Market: Distribution channels, sales motion, geographic presence
- Technology Stack: Known tools, platforms, patents, engineering blog
- Customers: Public references, case studies, logo count
- Partnerships: Key alliances, integrations, resellers
```

**Checklist: Profile Completeness**
- [ ] Financials sourced and dated (pitch decks, Crunchbase, filings)
- [ ] Leadership verified on LinkedIn/company site
- [ ] Product offering confirmed via website + 3rd-party reviews (G2, TrustRadius)
- [ ] Pricing sourced (website, sales calls, customer interviews)
- [ ] Tech stack inferred from job postings, engineering blog, BuiltWith/Wappalyzer

## SWOT Framework

For each competitor, run a disciplined SWOT analysis. Avoid the common trap of generic, unsubstantiated entries — every claim must be evidence-backed.

**Strengths** — What do they do better than anyone? (cite specific metrics, features, or market position)

**Weaknesses** — Where do they underperform? (product gaps, churn signals, Glassdoor reviews, support complaints)

**Opportunities** — What market shifts could they exploit? (regulatory changes, technology shifts, competitor exits)

**Threats** — What could erode their position? (new entrants, substitute products, pricing pressure, talent drain)

## Market Share Estimation

When direct market share data is unavailable, triangulate using proxy methods:

1. **Revenue-based:** Public filings → extract segment revenue. For private companies, estimate from employee count × industry revenue-per-employee benchmarks.
2. **Customer-count:** LinkedIn employee count by function, customer logos, job-postings analysis.
3. **Web-traffic proxy:** SimilarWeb / SEMrush for relative traffic share (treat as directional, not definitive).
4. **Hiring velocity:** Track job openings over time as a growth proxy signal.

Always note your estimation method, confidence level, and margin of error. Never present triangulated estimates as verified facts.

## Pricing Intelligence

Gather pricing data through layered sources and flag the reliability level:

| Method | Reliability | Notes |
|--------|------------|-------|
| Published pricing page | High | Snapshot only — may not reflect enterprise/negotiated pricing |
| Sales call / demo | Medium-High | Record exact date and context |
| Customer interviews | Medium | May reflect grandfathered pricing |
| Third-party review sites | Medium-Low | Often outdated |
| Job postings (comp) | Low (indirect) | Signals cost structure, not pricing |

## Signal Detection

Monitor competitors continuously for strategic signals. Build a signal catalog:

- **Product signals:** New feature launches, beta programs, pricing changes, API deprecations
- **People signals:** Key executive hires/departures, sudden hiring spikes in specific functions, layoffs
- **Financial signals:** Funding rounds, acquisition rumors, office expansions/contractions
- **Messaging signals:** Website copy changes, new positioning statements, PR agency changes
- **Legal signals:** Patent filings, trademark registrations, lawsuits, regulatory actions

### Monitoring Tactics

- Set up Google Alerts for competitor names + key executives
- Monitor competitor careers pages weekly (Wayback Machine for historical diffs)
- Track competitor blog RSS feeds and changelogs
- Subscribe to competitor newsletters from a non-company email
- Use BuiltWith/Wappalyzer to detect technology stack changes

**CI Ethics Note:** All intelligence gathering must use publicly available information and legal methods. Never misrepresent your identity, access proprietary systems, or induce third parties to breach confidentiality agreements.
