## Side Hustle Declaration Rules

[YOUR_BUSINESS_ALT] income is **trade income** reported under "Self-Employed Income" on Form B/B1.

- Registration: Sole proprietorship via ACRA (BizFile). UEN issued immediately.
- Reporting: 2-line statement — Revenue, Adjusted Profit/Loss. If revenue ≥ $500k, 4-line and certified accounts required.
- Filing deadline: 15 Apr (paper) / 18 Apr (e-filing) after YA.
- No separate corporate filing — profits taxed at personal rates.
- Income test: IRAS uses "badges of trade" (frequency, profit motive, scale). If side hustle has systematic revenue, it's taxable income — not hobby income.
- GST registration: required only if taxable turnover exceeds S$1M in 12 months.

[IRAS, Basic Guide for Self-Employed Persons]

## Sole Proprietorship vs Pte Ltd — Comparison

| Factor | Sole Prop | Pte Ltd |
|---|---|---|
| Tax rate | Personal progressive (0–24%) | Flat 17% corporate + dividend tax-free |
| Filing complexity | 2-line P&L on Form B | Full financials + ACRA annual return + ECI |
| Startup cost | ~$115 (ACRA 1yr registration) | ~$315 + company secretary (~$500/yr) |
| Liability | Unlimited personal | Limited to share capital |
| CPF for owner | Medisave only (voluntary) | Director's salary → mandatory CPF |
| Capital allowances | Yes (S19/S19A) | Yes (same rules) |
| R&R deduction (S14N) | Yes, up to $300k over 3 YAs | Yes, same cap |
| Profit extraction | All taxed in year earned | Salary + dividends (timing control) |
| Best for | Revenue < $200k, simple ops | Revenue > $200k, liability risk, profit retention |

- Rule of thumb: If [YOUR_BUSINESS_ALT] net profit exceeds ~$150k/yr and you want to retain earnings, Pte Ltd's 17% flat rate beats the 15%+ personal bracket.
- Pte Ltd lets you time profit extraction across years — sole prop taxes you on everything in the year earned.

## Deductible [YOUR_BUSINESS_ALT] Expenses

**Allowed (revenue in nature, wholly & exclusively for trade):**
- Hardware & materials (cost of goods sold — deduct in year incurred)
- Subcontractor/labour fees
- Transport (public, taxi, Grab for site visits — keep log)
- Marketing (Carousell ads, Facebook ads, website hosting)
- Phone/internet (business-use % only)
- Tools & consumables (drills, testers, cabling — claim in year of purchase if unit cost < $5k; else capital allowance)
- Home office (pro-rata floor area × utilities if used exclusively and regularly for business)
- Professional fees (accounting, ACRA renewal)

**Capital Allowances (equipment depreciation replacement):**
- Capital allowance under S19A: 100% write-off in year of purchase for assets costing ≤ $5,000 each (total capped at $30,000 per YA)
- Above threshold: 3-year straight-line (33.3% per year) or 1-year for computers/IT equipment
- Assets: laptops, test equipment, power tools, ladders, cable testers, smart home demo kit
- **Do NOT claim accounting depreciation on tax form** — use capital allowances schedule instead.

[IRAS, Capital Allowances S19/S19A]

## Estimated Chargeable Income Calculation

```
(A) Employment income (gross salary + AWS + bonus)
(B) Trade income ([YOUR_BUSINESS_ALT] revenue)
(C) Less: Allowable business expenses
(D) = (B) − (C) = Adjusted trade profit/(loss)
(E) Less: Capital allowances (equipment S19A)
(F) = (A) + (D) − (E) = Assessable income
(G) Less: Personal reliefs (Earned Income, CPF, SRS, etc.)
(H) = (F) − (G) = Chargeable income → apply tax brackets
```

- Personal reliefs: Earned Income Relief ($1,000 capped), CPF Relief (compulsory employee contributions), SRS contributions (capped at $15,300/yr Singaporean), NSman Relief ($1,500–$3,500), parent/handicapped relief.
- **Relief cap reminder:** All reliefs combined ≤ $80,000.

## Renovation-Specific Deductions (Section 14N)

For [YOUR_BUSINESS_ALT] projects done on **own business premises** (not client sites):
- Qualifying R&R: painting, flooring, partitions, electrical works, lighting, sanitary fittings, water installations.
- **Not qualifying:** structural works, designer fees, whole-office furniture.
- Deduction cap: $300,000 total, spread equally over 3 consecutive YAs ($100k/YA).
- Claim in Form B under "Allowable Business Expenses" in year costs first incurred.

For client-site renovation: These are **cost of goods sold** items for [YOUR_BUSINESS_ALT] — deduct fully in the year incurred as direct project costs. Section 14N does not apply.
