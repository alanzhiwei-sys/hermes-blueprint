## Example 1 — Short-Form Vertical Video Script (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)

**Context:** [NAME_REDACTED] says: "I need a 60-second TikTok script for [YOUR_BUSINESS]. Topic: why your smart home keeps disconnecting."

### ❌ Weak Response

> "Hi everyone! Today I'm going to talk about why smart homes disconnect. It's usually because of WiFi. You need a good router. Ubiquiti is great for this. Like and follow for more tips!"

*Why it fails:* No hook. No retention mechanics. Generic advice. Sounds like a corporate training video. Zero rewatch value. No pattern interrupt.

### ✅ Gold Standard Response

## TikTok Script — "Why Your Smart Home Keeps Disconnecting"

**Duration:** 45–55 seconds
**Format:** Talking head (selfie mode) + B-roll cuts
**Tone:** Frustrated-to-relieved energy arc

---

### The Script

```
[HOOK — 0:00–0:03]
🎬 ON-SCREEN TEXT: "Your smart home isn't broken."

[Face camera, slight frustration]
"You just bought a $3,000 smart home setup —
and your lights won't turn on half the time."

---

[PATTERN INTERRUPT — 0:03–0:06]
🎬 CUT TO: Phone screen showing "Device Unavailable" error
"It's not the devices. It's not the app.
It's the one thing NOBODY tells you about."

---

[BODY — 0:06–0:35]
🎬 CUT TO: Selfie mode, walking toward WiFi router
"Most smart home installers in Singapore
give you basic consumer routers.

The problem? Those routers handle ~30 devices.
A typical smart home has 50–80.

Every light bulb, sensor, and switch
is fighting for airtime.

When the router's CPU maxes out —
*poof* — your bedroom lights go offline."

🎬 CUT TO: Close-up of Ubiquiti UniFi Dream Machine
"This is what hotels and offices use.
It handles 300+ devices without breaking a sweat.

VLAN segmentation keeps your IoT devices
on their own lane — so your Netflix
never competes with your door sensor."

---

[PAYOFF — 0:35–0:45]
🎬 CUT TO: Selfie mode, calm energy
"A $400 router upgrade fixes 90%
of 'broken smart home' complaints.

Your devices were never the problem.
Your foundation was."

---

[CTA — 0:45–0:50]
🎬 ON-SCREEN: "[YOUR_BUSINESS] — We fix the foundation."

"Save this for when your installer
tries to sell you more devices
instead of a better network.

Drop a 🔌 in the comments if this happened to you."
```

---

### Production Notes

| Element | Spec |
|---|---|
| **Hook type** | "Truth Bomb" — contradicts common belief ("your smart home isn't broken") |
| **Retention mechanic** | Pattern interrupt at 3s (cut to error screen) + curiosity gap at 6s ("the one thing nobody tells you") |
| **Energy arc** | Frustrated → Technical → Confident → Empathetic |
| **B-roll** | Record separately: phone error screen, walking-to-router shot, UniFi close-up |
| **Captions** | Burn in auto-captions (85% watch without sound) |
| **Music** | No music during body — just during hook + CTA. Low-fi beat, ~90 BPM |

---

### Why This Works

1. **Hook in first 3 seconds** — no "hey guys welcome to my channel." The first sentence IS the hook.
2. **Pattern interrupt at 3s** — the cut to the error screen keeps the scroll-stopper alive past the critical 3-second retention cliff.
3. **Curiosity gap** — "the one thing nobody tells you about" makes them stay for the answer.
4. **"They" vs "You" framing** — the installer is the villain. The viewer is the smart one who figured it out. This is a proven persuasion pattern.
5. **Specific numbers** — "30 devices vs 50–80," "$400 router," "90% of complaints." Vague = skippable. Specific = credible.
6. **Save + Comment CTA** — not "like and follow." "Save" signals TikTok's algorithm that this is valuable. The emoji comment prompt drives engagement.

## Example 2 — Instagram Carousel Design Brief

**Context:** [NAME_REDACTED] says: "Make an Instagram carousel for [YOUR_COMPANY] about HDB renovation horror stories and how to avoid them."

### ❌ Weak Response

> "Slide 1: Title. Slide 2–4: Common mistakes. Slide 5: Call to action. Use nice photos."

*Why it fails:* No slide-by-slide breakdown. No hook psychology. No copy written. Just a structure outline — [NAME_REDACTED] still has to do all the work.

### ✅ Gold Standard Response

## Instagram Carousel — "Renovation Horror Stories (And How to Dodge Them)"

**Format:** 7-slide carousel
**Goal:** Education → Trust → DM inquiry
**Design style:** Warning yellow + dark contrast. Before/after photo pairs.

---

### Slide-by-Slide Breakdown

**Slide 1 — The Hook**
```
[IMAGE: Cracked tile close-up. Stark lighting.]

TOP TEXT (bold, white on dark overlay):
"3 Renovation Nightmares
  We Had to Rescue in 2026"

BOTTOM TEXT (smaller):
"(And exactly how to avoid them
  when you renovate YOUR flat)"
```
**Why:** Specific number ("3") + social proof ("we had to rescue") + direct address ("YOUR flat"). The "rescue" framing positions HFH as the fixer, not just another contractor.

---

**Slide 2 — Horror Story #1**
```
[IMAGE: Bathroom with water damage at base of wall]

HEADLINE: "The $8,000 Waterproofing Fix"
BODY: "Client hired cheapest quote ($2,800 for 2 toilets).
       6 months later: water seepage through bedroom wall.
       No waterproofing membrane installed.
       We had to hack everything and redo.
       
       Final cost: $8,200."
       
CALLOUT (red box): "Proper waterproofing = $1,200/toilet.
                      Skipping it = $4,000+/toilet to fix later."
```
**Why:** Named price creates stakes. The "$2,800 → $8,200" is a powerful visual. Red callout box creates scannable takeaway.

---

**Slide 3 — Horror Story #2**
```
[IMAGE: Uneven vinyl flooring with visible gaps]

HEADLINE: "The 'Self-Leveling? What's That?' Floor"
BODY: "Client's previous contractor laid vinyl
       directly over uneven cement screed.
       
       Result: Gaps appeared in 3 weeks.
       Every step made a hollow *thock* sound.
       
       We pulled up the entire floor.
       Self-leveled properly. Re-laid.
       
       Delay: +2 weeks. Extra cost: +$1,500."
       
CALLOUT (green box): "Always ask: 'Will you do self-leveling
                       compound before laying vinyl?'
                       If they hesitate — walk."
```
**Why:** The quotation tactic gives the reader a literal script. Social proof shifts from "we're good" to "here's the question that separates pros from cowboys."

---

**Slide 4 — Horror Story #3**
```
[IMAGE: Kitchen cabinet door hanging crooked]

HEADLINE: "The $0 Carpentry Quote (That Cost $4,500)"
BODY: "Carpenter quoted 'free design consultation.'
       Client signed without seeing material samples.
       
       Installed: 12mm chipboard (industry standard: 18mm).
       Doors warped in 6 months from SG humidity.
       
       We replaced all cabinets with 18mm plywood.
       Proper Blum hinges. Soft-close everything."
       
CALLOUT (yellow box): "Spec the thickness in your contract.
                        12mm ≠ 18mm. Don't let them swap."
```
**Why:** "Free" as a red flag is a powerful hook. Material specs ("12mm vs 18mm") make HFH look obsessive about details — that's the brand.

---

**Slide 5 — The Pattern**
```
[IMAGE: Simple infographic — three icons]

HEADLINE: "Notice the Pattern?"

[Icon 1: Magnifying glass on contract]
"The cheapest quote was never the cheapest."

[Icon 2: Shield with checkmark]
"Every rescue was a specification problem,
 not a skill problem."

[Icon 3: Handshake]
"You don't need to be a reno expert.
 You need a contractor who specs everything
 before they start."
```
**Why:** Synthesis slide. Moves from story → principle. Teaches the reader to think, not just to hire HFH.

---

**Slide 6 — The HFH Difference**
```
[IMAGE: Side-by-side — messy worksite (LEFT) vs clean HFH worksite (RIGHT)]

HEADLINE: "How We Build Differently"

BULLETS:
• Every material spec in writing before deposit
• Progress photos sent to your Telegram every 2 days
• Defects liability period: 12 months (industry: 6 months)
• WhatsApp group: you + site supervisor + PM (zero telephone game)
```
**Why:** Specific differentiators, not vague claims. The Telegram/WhatsApp bullet is a unique operational detail — competitors can't copy it without changing their process.

---

**Slide 7 — CTA**
```
[IMAGE: HFH team photo — smiling, on-site, hard hats]

HEADLINE: "Your Flat. Done Right. The First Time."

BODY: "Planning a renovation in 2026?
       DM us 'CHECKLIST' for our free
       12-Point Pre-Reno Inspection Guide."

SMALL TEXT (bottom): "@hoongfatheng | 991A Upp [LOCATION_REDACTED] | 322F [LOCATION_REDACTED]"
```

---

### Design Specs

| Element | Value |
|---|---|
| **Aspect ratio** | 1:1 (1080×1080) |
| **Color palette** | #1A1A1A (dark bg), #FFD700 (yellow accents), #FFFFFF (text), #FF4444 (warning boxes) |
| **Font** | Inter Bold (headlines), Inter Regular (body) |
| **Photo treatment** | All photos: slight desaturation + vignette. HFH brand photos: warm, natural light |

---

### Why This Works

1. **Named prices on every slide** — renovation content that doesn't talk money is invisible. Specific numbers trigger the "this is real" response.
2. **"Rescue" narrative** — HFH isn't selling renovation. HFH is selling "we fix other people's mistakes." Higher trust, higher perceived value.
3. **Reader empowerment** — slide 3 and 4 give literal scripts/questions the reader can use. This builds trust before any sale.
4. **Pattern recognition (slide 5)** — teaches the reader a mental model, not just facts. They'll remember the pattern and associate it with HFH.
5. **CTA provides value first** — "DM for checklist" is a micro-commitment. Way lower friction than "DM for quote." Once they're in DMs, the conversation opens naturally.

## Example 3 — Batch Content Calendar (Monthly)

**Context:** [NAME_REDACTED] says: "Plan a month of content for [YOUR_BUSINESS]. Mix of educational + behind-the-scenes + client results."

### ❌ Weak Response

> "Week 1: Smart home tips. Week 2: Client showcase. Week 3: Product review. Week 4: Q&A."

*Why it fails:* No hooks. No production specs. No posting cadence. No platform-specific formats. Just topic labels.

### ✅ Gold Standard Response

## [YOUR_BUSINESS] — June 2026 Content Calendar

**Platforms:** TikTok (primary), Instagram Reels (cross-post), LinkedIn (long-form)
**Cadence:** 3 posts/week (Mon / Wed / Fri)
**Total:** 12 posts for June
**Pillar split:** 40% Educational / 30% Social Proof / 20% Behind-the-Scenes / 10% Personal Brand

---

### Week 1 — Foundation Week (Network Education)

| Day | Pillar | Topic | Format | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Educational | "Why your 2.4GHz Zigbee keeps clashing with WiFi" | 45s talking head + diagram overlay | "Your smart home has a traffic jam you can't see." |
| **Wed** | Behind-the-Scenes | "Unboxing: UniFi Dream Wall — is it worth $1,200?" | 60s unboxing + first impressions | "This is either the best router in Singapore — or the most overpriced." |
| **Fri** | Social Proof | "Client: 4-room BTO. 52 devices. Zero disconnects for 6 months." | 30s walkthrough B-roll + testimonial text overlay | "They were told 'smart homes are unreliable.' We proved otherwise." |

---

### Week 2 — Pain Point Week (Problem → Solution)

| Day | Pillar | Topic | Format | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Educational | "Your NAS is a sitting duck — 3 security settings you haven't enabled" | 50s screen recording + voiceover | "That Synology you bought? It's broadcasting to the internet right now." |
| **Wed** | Educational | "Home Assistant vs Google Home: when to use which" | 60s split-screen comparison | "One is a remote control. The other is a brain. Here's the difference." |
| **Fri** | Social Proof | "Client: Landed property. 4 floors. Full UniFi + HA + CCTV. Walkthrough." | 90s cinematic walkthrough (gimbal footage) | "What does a $12,000 smart home actually look like?" |

---

### Week 3 — Authority Week (Deep Knowledge)

| Day | Pillar | Topic | Format | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Educational | "VLANs for dummies — why your smart home needs network segregation" | 60s whiteboard-style explainer | "Your smart light bulb is spying on your laptop. Here's how." |
| **Wed** | Personal Brand | "How I Got Here: from IT guy to smart home founder" | 60s storytelling (talking head) | "5 years ago I was fixing printers. Now I design networks for million-dollar homes." |
| **Fri** | Social Proof | "Before/After: client's WiFi dead zones → full UniFi coverage. Speed test comparison." | 40s split-screen (before speed test / after speed test) | "They went from 12 Mbps in the master bedroom to 800 Mbps." |

---

### Week 4 — Decision Week (Conversion)

| Day | Pillar | Topic | Format | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | Educational | "Smart home packages: $1,500 vs $4,000 vs $8,000 — what's the real difference?" | 75s tier comparison | "The price difference isn't the hardware. It's the architecture." |
| **Wed** | Behind-the-Scenes | "A day in the life: installing a full UniFi network in a 5-room HDB" | 90s vlog (time-lapse + voiceover) | "This is what 8 hours of cable management looks like in 90 seconds." |
| **Fri** | Conversion | "The 5 questions to ask ANY smart home installer before signing" | 50s checklist + text overlay | "If they can't answer #3, walk away immediately." |

---

### Production Schedule

| Task | Frequency | Owner | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Film batch** | Every Sunday | [NAME_REDACTED] (talking head + B-roll) | 2 hours |
| **Edit + caption** | Sunday evening | Outsource (CapCut template) | 1 hour |
| **Schedule posts** | Sunday night | Later.com auto-publish | 15 min |
| **Engage comments** | Daily, 15 min | [NAME_REDACTED] | Mon–Fri 8 AM |

---

### Why This Works

1. **Each post has a hook written, not just a topic** — [NAME_REDACTED] can film immediately. Zero creative friction.
2. **Pillar rotation** — educational → social proof → personal brand. Never two of the same type back-to-back. Prevents content fatigue.
3. **Week themes** — each week has a narrative arc. Feels like a series, not random posts.
4. **Conversion week at month-end** — after 3 weeks of value, the audience is primed. Week 4 posts naturally lead to inquiries.
5. **Production schedule included** — not just "what to post" but "when to film, edit, and schedule." Content calendars fail when logistics aren't baked in.

---

## What Makes a Good Apollo Example

| Quality | Bad | Good |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook quality** | "Today I'll talk about..." | First sentence IS the hook. Pattern interrupt. Curiosity gap. |
| **Specificity** | "Use better equipment" | "$400 Ubiquiti router replaces your ISP box" |
| **Platform awareness** | Same content for all platforms | TikTok: hook-first. Carousel: slide-by-slide. LinkedIn: long-form narrative. |
| **CTA design** | "Like and follow" | "Save + comment 🔌" (algorithm-friendly). "DM for checklist" (conversion funnel). |
| **Production specs** | No instructions | B-roll list, BPM, captions on/off, aspect ratio, color palette |
| **Batch efficiency** | One-off posts | Calendar with film-batch schedule, edit templates, auto-publishing |
