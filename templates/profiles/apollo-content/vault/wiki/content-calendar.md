# Content Calendar — Apollo Content

## Calendar Philosophy

The Apollo content calendar is not a straitjacket — it's a **rhythm keeper**. We plan 80% of content in advance and leave 20% for reactive, time-sensitive posts (news, trends, audience replies that deserve a full post). This ratio prevents both creative chaos and the sterile feeling of an over-scheduled feed.

## Weekly Structure

Every week follows a five-day publishing cadence. Weekends are for planning and batch production, not for publishing (exceptions: scheduled Reels and TikTok auto-posts).

### Monday — Educational Deep Dive
- **Format:** LinkedIn text post (1,200–2,000 words) or carousel (8–12 slides)
- **Goal:** High save rate. This is the pillar content the rest of the week repurposes.
- **Prep:** Draft completed the previous Friday. Final polish Monday morning before 9am publish.

### Tuesday — Engagement / Conversation
- **Format:** LinkedIn poll or open-ended question; X thread on the same topic
- **Goal:** Comment volume. The question should be genuinely debatable — not a "who agrees?" softball.
- **Prep:** Write the question + your own hot-take reply (publish the reply 2 hours after the poll).

### Wednesday — Story / Case Study
- **Format:** Instagram carousel or LinkedIn narrative post with a specific, named example
- **Goal:** Share rate. Stories travel further than advice.
- **Prep:** Pull from the case study bank maintained in `vault/raw/case-studies/`. Refresh at least one case study per month.

### Thursday — Commentary / Perspective
- **Format:** LinkedIn text post (800–1,200 words) reacting to an industry event, trend, or contrarian take
- **Goal:** Profile visits and inbound connection requests
- **Prep:** Monitor News API and Twitter lists Monday-Wednesday for commentary fodder.

### Friday — Curated Wrap + Newsletter
- **Format:** LinkedIn "3 things I learned this week" post + email newsletter
- **Goal:** Link clicks, newsletter growth
- **Prep:** Compile throughout the week in a running Notion doc. Include one external link per item.

## Monthly & Seasonal Planning

### Monthly Theme

Each month gets a **content theme** that unifies the four weekly deep dives. Themes rotate quarterly:

| Month | Theme Example |
|---|---|
| January | Annual planning & goal setting |
| February | Brand storytelling |
| March | Audience growth tactics |
| April | Content monetisation |
| May | Visual & design strategy |
| June | Mid-year audit & course correction |

One month = one pillar topic explored from four angles (strategy, execution, measurement, tools).

### Seasonal Hooks

Map these annually and prep content 2-3 weeks in advance:

- **Q1:** New Year reset content, annual trend predictions, Valentine's Day (relationship with your audience angle)
- **Q2:** Tax season (freelancer tips), graduation season (career content), mid-year reflection
- **Q3:** Back-to-school (learning content), summer slowdown (maintenance & review), Black Friday prep (October)
- **Q4:** Black Friday / Cyber Monday (offer content), annual wrap-ups, holiday scheduling (lighter posting)

## Batch Production

**Monday morning is sacred production time.** Block 3 hours every Monday to:

1. Write the week's three LinkedIn text posts (Mon, Wed, Thu deep dives)
2. Record the week's Reel (or batch two if inspiration strikes)
3. Design carousels using the Figma template library
4. Schedule everything in Buffer/Hootsuite for the week ahead

**Repurposing pipeline — one asset, five forms:**

| Source Asset | Repurposed Into |
|---|---|
| Monday LinkedIn post | → Instagram carousel (Wed) |
| Monday LinkedIn post | → Twitter thread (Tue) |
| Monday LinkedIn post | → YouTube short (following week) |
| Thursday commentary | → Newsletter segment (Fri) |
| Monthly deep dive | → YouTube long-form (month-end) |

Never create content from scratch for a secondary platform. Always start with the primary asset (usually the LinkedIn post) and adapt.

## Content Bank

Maintain a **content idea bank** with at least 30 ready-to-write prompts. Sources:

- Questions from comment sections (tag with `#from-comments`)
- Competitor content gaps ("nobody is talking about X")
- Keyword research (low-competition, high-intent phrases)
- Personal experiences and lessons learned
- Client conversations (anonymised, with permission)

Review the bank during the Sunday planning session. Depleted ideas get replenished from the same sources throughout the week.

## Editorial Workflow

```
Idea → Draft (Mon-Wed prior week) → Review (Thu) → Polish (Fri) → Schedule (Sun) → Publish → Monitor (24h post-publish) → Archive learnings
```

Every published post gets a 24-hour check-in: record saves, shares, comments, and one qualitative note ("landed well because..." or "flopped likely because..."). These notes feed the quarterly audit.
