# Brand Guidelines — Apollo Content

## Brand Voice

Apollo Content speaks with **warm authority**. We are knowledgeable but never pedantic, friendly but never sloppy, and aspirational but never pretentious. Every piece of communication should leave the reader feeling **informed, respected, and motivated to act**.

**Core voice attributes:**
- **Confident** — State opinions clearly. Hedge only when the data is ambiguous.
- **Conversational** — Write like you're explaining something to a smart friend over coffee. Contractions are welcome.
- **Precise** — Use specific numbers, dates, and names. Vague language ("many people," "some time ago") erodes trust.
- **Generous** — Share what you know. Don't gatekeep. Every post should give the reader a takeaway they can use today.

**Tone spectrum — same voice, different volume:**

| Context | Tone Shift |
|---|---|
| Educational blog post | Patient, structured, uses examples |
| Product launch | Energetic, benefit-forward, short paragraphs |
| Apology / correction | Direct, no fluff, clear timeline for fix |
| Social reply | Warm, brief, occasionally playful |
| Case study | Factual, chronological, data-rich |

## Visual Identity

### Colour Palette

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Apollo Navy | `#1A1A2E` | Primary text, headers, dark backgrounds |
| Solar Gold | `#E94560` | CTAs, highlights, accent borders |
| Cloud White | `#F8F9FA` | Page backgrounds, negative space |
| Slate | `#6C757D` | Secondary text, captions, muted elements |
| Aegean Teal | `#0F3460` | Hover states, secondary accents, data visualisation |

**Golden rule:** Never tint the Solar Gold. Use it at 100% opacity or not at all. Gold at 80% reads as muddy orange.

### Typography

- **Display / Headlines:** *Inter* — weights 700 (h1), 600 (h2), 500 (h3). Always use `-0.02em` letter-spacing on headlines above 24px.
- **Body:** *Source Serif 4* — weight 400, line-height 1.6. Our serif body choice signals thoughtfulness and differentiates us from the sans-serif sea.
- **Code / data:** *JetBrains Mono* — weight 400, reserved for technical asides, code blocks, and numeric tables.
- **Fallback stack:** `Inter, system-ui, sans-serif` for display; `Source Serif 4, Georgia, serif` for body.

**Type scale (px):** 12 / 14 / 16 / 18 / 20 / 24 / 32 / 40 / 56

### Logo Rules

- The Apollo wordmark is set in **Inter Bold** with the "A" glyph customised — the crossbar is replaced with a subtle horizon line.
- **Minimum clear space:** Equal to the height of the "A" on all four sides.
- **Minimum size:** Never render the logo smaller than 32px wide for digital, or 15mm for print.
- **Don'ts:** No outlines, no drop shadows, no rotation, no placing on busy photographic backgrounds without a scrim. Never change the logo colour — it is always Apollo Navy (`#1A1A2E`) or Cloud White (`#F8F9FA`) on dark backgrounds.

## Imagery & Photography

- **Style:** High-contrast, natural light. Avoid obviously stock-photo tropes (person pointing at whiteboard, handshake in lobby).
- **Colour treatment:** Desaturate secondary colours by 15%. Protect skin tones.
- **Illustration:** Line-art style, single weight (2px stroke), using only palette colours.

## Tone Examples

**Good (educational):**
> "The fastest way to kill a launch post is to bury the benefit. Your reader has 1.5 seconds to decide whether to scroll past. Lead with what they get, not what you built."

**Good (social reply):**
> "Great question — and honestly, we made this mistake too. Here's the one-line fix: swap your headline and your CTA. Readers scan the headline first. Make it earn the click."

**Bad (too formal):**
> "It is recommended that organisations consider the implementation of benefit-forward copywriting methodologies to optimise engagement metrics."

**Bad (too casual):**
> "yo just put the good stuff first lol it works trust me"
